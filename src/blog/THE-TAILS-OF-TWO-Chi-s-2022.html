THE TAILS OF TWO CHI’s
THE TAILS OF TWO Chi's
Bonnie, the brown and white chi, came to the attention of our Rescue
from a long-time volunteer C, who negotiated to save her from the county
shelter in a highly depressed, very low income neighborhood. Bonnie-girl,
the elderly chi, arrived at the shelter door as a stray, absolutely COVERED
with hideous fatty tumors, amongst numerous other issues. After we
provided her $urgery, her stitches were the FULL LENGTH of her tiny
underside (see photo!)
Short version of her complex story: We were able to facilitate Bonnie's
surgery, spay & additional medical treatment to make her eligible to be
adoptable. P and family agreed to foster her under temporary emergency
conditions. They all fell in love with her during her recovery, even P's
OTHER dogs love Bonnie! And now they have confirmed that Bonnie is safe
with P and company FUREVER!
"The Second Tale:"
Two weeks before Bonnie came to us, one of our sister Rescues in the back
country of Orange County contacted us about Sasha, a one year old
Black and white chi. The grandmother of the family had adopted her at 8
weeks old. Unfortunately right after Sasha's first birthday, Grandma allowed
her 5 year old granddaughter to play with Sasha unsupervised. The child
inadvertently dropped the pup off the bed and broke the dog’s leg!
The leg still had not healed months later when it was decided that Grandma
needed to go back East to a senior assisted living facility that would not
allow her to bring any pets, even healthy ones. In fact, because the cast was
on for so long, the second front leg ALSO developed fracture! No rescue
they approached wanted to take on the potentially enormous expense and
delicate rehabilitation and recovery of the tiny little girl Chihuahua. The
grandma's vet had advocated removal of the first leg altogether as the
"easiest" solution.
Long story short- we were able to intake Sasha, transport to San Diego,
have her seen by more experienced vets and physical therapists. The leg
was saved, physical therapy was followed up, the cast removed & Sasha
ADOPTED into a loving family with two older teenage girls and a companion
small dog whom she adores! Sasha now runs around ALL DAY LONG
INDOORS on ALL four HEALTHY legs!
And so, we here at ARC Rescue echo Dicken's heroic protagonist, Sydney
Carton: 'It is a far, far better thing that I do, than I have ever
done; it is a far, far better rest that I go to than I have ever
known."
